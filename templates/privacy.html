Privacy Policy

Last Updated: September 29, 2025

FAMJAM ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and share information about you when you use our Service.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information in the following ways:

  • Parent Account Information: When a Parent creates an account, we collect a username, email address, and a hashed password.
  • Child Account Information: When a Child account is created via a Parent's invitation, we only collect a username and a hashed password. We do not request or store email addresses or other personal contact information for Child accounts.
  • User-Generated Content: We collect the data you and your family members generate while using the service. This includes chores, habits, rewards, challenges, family timers, personal notes, to-do lists, and mood entries (including emoji, score, and optional notes).
  • Automatically Collected Information: We may collect basic information about your device and usage, but we do not use tracking cookies for advertising purposes.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Service.
  • Allow family members to view and interact with shared family content, such as chore lists, calendars, challenges, and leaderboards.
  • Enable personal features, such as private notes and to-do lists, which are only visible to the user who created them.
  • Process reward requests and update point balances.
  • Power our AI features, as detailed in the next section.

3. Data Sharing and Third-Party Services

We do not sell your personal information. We only share it in the following limited circumstances:

  • Within Your Family Group: Most content you create (chores, rewards, challenges, timers, family mood data) is shared with members of your designated family group to allow the Service to function. Personal notes and to-do lists are not shared.
  • With OpenAI for AI Features: To provide AI-powered features, we send specific data to OpenAI.
    • For the FamJam Plan generator, we send the family goal you provide and the first names (usernames) of your children.
    • For the AI Insights mood analysis, we send a log of the requesting user's mood entries, which includes the date, time of day, mood description, and any associated notes you have written.

    We use OpenAI's API services, and according to their policy, they do not use data submitted via their API to train their models. You can review their policies for more information: OpenAI Privacy Policy.

  • For Legal Reasons: We may share information if we believe disclosure is required by law or to protect the safety and rights of others.

4. Children's Privacy (COPPA Compliance)

We are committed to complying with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). Our Service is designed for families, and we recognize that children under the age of 13 may use it.

  • Parental Consent: We require a verifiable parent or legal guardian to create a family account and invite children. The act of creating a Parent account and inviting a child constitutes parental consent for the collection of the limited information described in this policy.
  • Limited Data Collection: We only collect a username from children. We do not collect their email address, physical address, phone number, or photos.
  • Parental Rights: As a parent, you have the right to:
    1. Review the personal information we have collected from your child.
    2. Request that we delete your child's personal information.
    3. Refuse to permit further collection or use of your child's information.
    To exercise these rights, please contact us at the email below. We will take steps to verify your identity as the parent before proceeding.

5. Data Security and Retention

We use industry-standard security measures, including hashing passwords with Bcrypt, to protect your data. We retain your information as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, we will take steps to delete all associated personal information from our systems.

6. Your Rights

You have the right to access, update, or delete your personal information. Parents can manage their children's accounts, including editing usernames or removing the account entirely, from their dashboard.

7. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at: oblivio.company@gmail.com.
